Malnutrition

Malnutrtion is a major issue among Syrian refugees. They are living in poverty whether they are displaced in their country or in an asylum country. With poverty comes lack of food and water. If children are malnutrioned for too long their bodies will fail them.


Syrians fled their homes in order to avoid the poverty and fighting that they had been facing, but since the countries they fled to are not equipped to deal with such a large refugee population, many Syrians end up suffering even after they escape the war zone.

Syrian refugees have limited access to food, clean water and healthcare causing an increase in diseases, malnutrition and other health issues.

Most of the babies born to women refugees are under six pounds and there is a lack of breastmilk production because the mothers are malnutritioned.

Resources

Many refugees are able to receive a short-term health insurance plan which lasts only 8 months. This is called Refugee Medial Assistance (RMA). With RMA, the refugee gets the same benefits package as a Medicaid recipient in that state. When the RMA expires (after 8 months), these refugees are treated the same as other low-income persons in their state in terms of their eligibility for medical insurance assistance. https://www.acf.hhs.gov/orr/programs/refugee-health

Interpreter Services

It is essential that health care and related services are provided in a language and manner that the refugee can understand. Federal laws, such as Title VI of the 1964 Civil Rights Act, require federal agencies to implement systems to help those with limited English proficiency. In addition, some states have passed laws requiring competent medical interpreters in all hospital and mental health emergency rooms.

Mental Health

Mental health is a big priority when it comes to refugees seeking helath care. They have been a part of a conflict that has affected, and may continue to affect, their everyday well being. A large majority of Syrian refugees are children. Post traumatic stress disorder is the most common health issue facing children. Many children are severely traumatized with symptoms of depression, withdrawl, regression, chronic anxiety, panic attacks, nightmares, and obsessive, compulsive, and reactive behaviors. In order to prevent further trauma mental healthcare is neccessary.

Children and adolescents often have higher levels with various investigations revealing rates of PTSD from 50-90% and major depression from 6-40%. Risk factors for the development of mental health problems include the number of traumas, delayed asylum application process, detention, and the loss of culture and support systems.
