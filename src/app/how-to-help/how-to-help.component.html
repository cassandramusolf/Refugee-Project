According to the U.N. in 2016, $4.5 billion dollars minimum was needed to meet the urgent needs of the most vulnerable of syrians, but only $2.9 billion was recieved. About 6 in 10 Syrains are now displaceds from their homes.

Syrian refugees still face hardships once fleeing from Syria. Finances: Many refugees state that it can be difficult to find jobs and legal ways to make a living, usually accepting low pay in order to provide resources for their families to survive. Shelter is hard to come by and many families live without heat or running water. Sanitation: lack of clean water and sanitation in crowded locations allows for diseases like cholera and polio to spread.
